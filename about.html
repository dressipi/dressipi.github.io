About Dressipi

Dressipi is an award-winning fashion technology business which focuses on helping customers manage their dressing and shopping lives, whilst giving brands the opportunity to influence and understand their customers.

Having invested in over 24 months of resource and finance building proprietary technology, Dressipi delivers recommendations that are context-aware and optimised for every customer, guiding them to the products they are most likely to buy and keep in their perfect size. This not only builds consumer purchasing confidence but also has a positive impact on retailer’s revenue, returns and conversion rates.

Dressipi boasts an impressive team, led by industry renowned technologists, and some of fashion’s most experienced stylists.

The founders

Sarah McVittie

Sarah is a serial entrepreneur and co-founder of Dressipi. She was named one of the Top Ten Most Influential People in the Fashion Business in the Hospital Club’s 2012 Hot100 and one of the 35 women entrepreneurs under 35 by The Times. Prior to starting Dressipi, Sarah launched and ran Texperts, the world’s first Q&A SMS service, which won Shell Livewire and Natwest Startups awards before its multimillion sale to KGB giants, 118118.

Donna North

Prior to Dressipi Donna was at IMG leading the build of media content distribution technologies and advising IMG companies and clients on their digital strategies in the areas of Fashion, TV and Sport. Multi-award winning, she has launched a number of ‘firsts’ across mobile and web.

The Technologists

Frederick Cheung

Having previously worked at Textperts with Sarah, Fred is Dressipi’s CTO, overseeing all things technical. He is the creator of Speed Download and the co-author of Emberlight. A contributor to Ruby on Rails, Fred is the proud winner of the 2012 Ruby Hero award.

Rob Bevan

Before Dressipi, Rob wrote building energy management software for Oxford University, developed campaigns for The Climate Group and co-created a BAFTA award-winning online drama.

The Stylists

Natalie Theo

Natalie’s fashion credentials were earned at Condé Nast launching Russian Vogue and Greek Vogue, where she was lucky enough to meet and interview everyone from Yves Saint Laurent to Karl Lagerfeld. She then spent seven whirlwind years at the Daily Mail as their Fashion Editor, getting to grips with the dressing habits of millions of female readers on a daily basis.

Tara Kavanagh-Dowsett

Tara began her styling career at The Daily Telegraph, having achieved a degree in Fashion Design at University of Westminster. She has contributed to many notable publications such as The Times, British Glamour and Sport & Street. As well as being a busy stylist, she also art directs for some of the best British clothing brands.
